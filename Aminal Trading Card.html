  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus familiaris
  • Average Length: Varies greatly depending on the breed. For example, small breeds like Chihuahuas can be as small as 15 cm, while large breeds like the Great Dane can be up to 86 cm tall.
  • Average Lifespan: On average, dogs live between 10 to 13 years, but this can vary significantly based on factors such as breed, size, and overall health.

Dogs are mammals of the order Carnivora, specifically belonging to the Canidae family. There are hundreds of different breeds of dogs, each with their own unique characteristics and traits. A group of dogs is often referred to as a pack, particularly in the context of wild or feral dogs. Dogs are renowned for their keen sense of smell, which is far superior to that of humans, and their loyalty to their human companions.
